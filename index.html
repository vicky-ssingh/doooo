DOI Information

Digital Object Identifiers (DOIs) are crucial in ensuring persistent access to digital content in academic publishing. Learn more about how DOIs work and their importance in the scholarly ecosystem.

Importance of DOIs

  • Persistent links for long-term accessibility
  • Simplified citation in academic references
  • Reliable access to published digital materials
  • Enhanced discoverability of research
  • Improved tracking of research impact

How Our Journal Uses DOIs

We systematically assign DOIs to all published articles, ensuring that each piece of research has a unique, persistent identifier. To find a DOI for any article published in our journal:

  1. Visit the article page on our website
  2. Locate the DOI below the article abstract
  3. Use the DOI to create a persistent link to the article

Benefits for Authors and Readers

For Authors

  • Enhanced discoverability through global DOI indexing
  • Simplified citations leading to higher academic recognition
  • Accurate tracking of article usage and impact
  • Persistent identification of research outputs

For Readers

  • Easy and reliable access to articles via DOI links
  • Simplified reference management
  • Ability to track article versions and updates
  • Enhanced interconnectivity between related research

DOI Registration and Management

We register DOIs through CrossRef, ensuring proper indexing and management of all our published content. This process involves:

  1. Assigning a unique DOI to each article upon acceptance
  2. Submitting article metadata to CrossRef
  3. Activating the DOI upon article publication
  4. Maintaining and updating DOI information as needed

FAQs about DOIs

What is a DOI?

A DOI (Digital Object Identifier) is a unique alphanumeric string assigned to digital objects like journal articles, providing a persistent link to their location on the internet.

How are DOIs registered?

DOIs are registered through registration agencies like CrossRef. Publishers submit metadata about their content, which is then assigned a unique DOI.

What if a DOI link breaks?

If a DOI link breaks, the publisher is responsible for updating the DOI's location. Users should still be able to access the content through the DOI system.

How to use a DOI for citation?

To cite using a DOI, include it at the end of your reference. For example: Author, A. (Year). Title. Journal Name, Volume(Issue), pp-pp. https://doi.org/10.xxxx/xxxxx
